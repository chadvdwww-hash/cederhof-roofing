Site-Wide Critical Findings

Every major issue identified across the 11 pages audited, ranked by severity. These are the exact gaps Cederhof can exploit to outrank the benchmark.

01 — Zero meta descriptions site-wide
Critical
None of the 11 audited pages has a meta name="description" tag. On a 150-page site that means Google auto-generates every SERP snippet from the first paragraph of body copy — lost click-through-rate control on every keyword HMC ranks for.
Cederhof play: Unique 150-160 character meta descriptions on every page. Primary keyword + service area + CTA phrase. Instant advantage across the entire site.
02 — NAP inconsistency: /gallery-2/ shows wrong phone number
Critical
The gallery page title shows phone 072 184 3432. Every other page uses 010 502-0627. Google's Name/Address/Phone (NAP) signal is a primary local-SEO ranking factor. A phone mismatch between site pages, and between site and Google Business Profile, actively damages local-pack rankings.
Cederhof play: Single canonical phone hardcoded in one Astro include — footer, header, schema, meta. Zero divergence. Matches GBP exactly.
03 — 100+ templated suburb pages create duplicate-content risk
High
Suburb pages (e.g. /roof-repairs-kempton-park/, /painting-contractors-bedfordview/) share structural patterns with only the suburb name swapped. Near-duplicate content competing for overlapping intent. Google's Helpful Content and Panda systems flag this pattern as low-value programmatic SEO.
Cederhof play: 8 to 10 deep unique suburb pages, each referencing real property types in that area, local architectural character, real completed projects nearby. Quality over HMC's quantity.
04 — JSON-LD schema coverage at 36%
High
Only 4 of 11 pages have any structured data. Zero pages have FAQPage, Review, or BreadcrumbList schema. Rich results in SERPs (star ratings, breadcrumbs, FAQ expansions) are entirely absent from HMC's search presence.
Cederhof play: LocalBusiness + Service + Review + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList JSON-LD on every page. Every FAQ block on every service page gets FAQPage markup.
05 — Open Graph / Twitter Cards: 36% / 0%
High
Four pages have Open Graph tags, none have Twitter Cards. When a Joburg homeowner shares an HMC page on WhatsApp (the primary SA sharing channel), the preview is ugly or blank. A branded og:image is the most under-valued social-SEO asset in SA local services.
Cederhof play: Complete OG + Twitter Card on every page. Branded 1200×630 og:image per service. Instantly more shareable than any competitor in the set.
06 — Image alt text averages 40%, drops to 14% on gallery
High
Alt coverage ranges from 14% on /gallery-2/ (180+ images, 25 with alt) to 75% on /complete-roof-restorations/. Roofing is visually led — Google Image Search drives meaningful lead flow in this category. Missing alt hurts both accessibility and image rankings. The gallery is the single biggest wasted ranking opportunity on the site.
Cederhof play: 100% descriptive alt text, keyword-natural. Gallery captions embed property type + suburb + material. "Slate roof replacement on Cape-Dutch home, Dainfern" ranks for three queries at once.
07 — Canonical tags missing on 64% of pages
Medium
Only 4 of 11 audited pages have a rel="canonical" tag. Combined with the 100+ templated suburb pages, this leaves the site vulnerable to duplicate-content interpretation by Google. Canonicals are a free hygiene signal.
Cederhof play: Self-referencing canonical on every page. One line in the Astro SEOHead component.
08 — Typo in homepage H2: "peope" instead of "people"
Medium
"Why more peope choose HMC Works" appears as an H2 on the homepage. Small but corrosive trust signal on a services site where craft and attention to detail are the product. A visitor who sees a typo on the homepage starts expecting typos in the contract.
Cederhof play: Copy is a craft surface. Zero typos, zero awkward phrasing. All content passes grammar review before deploy.
09 — Brand name capitalisation inconsistent
Medium
Titles and H1s alternate between "HMC WORKS", "HMC works", and "HMC Works". Looks careless in the SERP and confuses automated brand extraction.
Cederhof play: One brand string, one case style, applied site-wide via central config.
10 — No breadcrumb markup or navigation
Medium
Neither visual breadcrumbs nor BreadcrumbList JSON-LD are present. Google uses breadcrumb schema to display path information in SERPs and to understand site hierarchy — on a 150-page site this is a large lost orientation signal.
Cederhof play: Visual breadcrumbs on every non-home page, plus full BreadcrumbList schema.
11 — Title under-utilisation on some pages
Low
/roof-painting/ uses a 44-character title — 16 characters of SERP real estate left on the table. Title tags remain among the highest-leverage single ranking factors; every character should be earning.
Cederhof play: Every title targets 55-60 characters. Primary keyword leading, brand at the tail.
12 — No modern image formats or lazy loading
Medium
Site serves JPG/PNG only, no evidence of loading="lazy" or responsive srcset. On SA mobile data at R149-299/GB, page weight is a direct conversion cost. Core Web Vitals LCP and CLS will both underperform.
Cederhof play: Astro Image component auto-converts to WebP, generates srcset, lazy-loads below the fold. Hero images under 80KB.

05

Cannibalization & Templated Pages

The single most damaging strategic issue on the site. Over 100 suburb landing pages share an identical template with only the suburb name swapped.

Cluster A — "roof repairs [suburb]"
High risk
/roof-repairs-kempton-park/, /roof-repairs-boksburg/, /roof-repairs-benoni/, /roof-repairs-bedfordview/, /roof-repairs-centurion/, /roof-repairs-edenvale/, plus ~20 others — all use identical service descriptions, team structures, supplier logos, and portfolio placeholder text. Only the suburb name, a few area-specific sentences, and the H1/title change.
Cederhof play: Fewer pages, genuinely unique. Each suburb page references real completed projects in that area, local architectural style notes, real testimonials from residents of that suburb.
Cluster B — "painting contractors [suburb]"
High risk
Same pattern on the painting side. /painting-contractors-bedfordview/, /painting-contractors-benoni/, /painting-contractors-boksburg/ and ~25 others. Google's Helpful Content and Panda systems both flag this pattern as low-value programmatic SEO.
Cederhof play: Services and suburbs are two separate page types. Service pages go deep on the work. Suburb pages link to service pages. Each is independently valuable.

Recommended Action Plan for Cederhof

Six priorities in execution order. These become Phase 0.5 and Phase 2 requirements for the Cederhof build — they do not belong in a "later" list.

01
Ship meta descriptions on every page — immediately
Unique, 150-160 chars, primary keyword + service area + CTA. Template-driven from the brief's keyword map so every page gets one. HMC has 0%, Cederhof ships 100%.
Impact: Full SERP snippet control site-wide. Est. 15-30% CTR uplift on ranked keywords.
02
Full schema stack on every page
LocalBusiness + Service + Review + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList. Built into the Astro SEOHead component. Every FAQ block auto-wraps in FAQPage markup.
Impact: Rich SERP results (stars, breadcrumbs, FAQ expansions) on every indexed page.
03
Deep unique suburb pages — 8 not 100
Dainfern, Sandton, Bryanston, Hyde Park, Fourways, Rivonia, Hurlingham, Parkhurst. Each page 1,200+ words of genuinely unique content. Real completed projects in that suburb. Local architectural context. Zero template duplication.
Impact: Premium residential positioning + ranks for "[service] [suburb]" across 8 high-value Joburg North searches.
04
Complete social card stack
Open Graph + Twitter Card on every page. Branded 1200×630 og:image per service. When a Dainfern homeowner shares a Cederhof page on WhatsApp, the preview is branded and premium.
Impact: Beats every competitor's social-share quality from day one.
05
Perfect alt text + WebP images
100% alt coverage with descriptive, keyword-natural captions. All images via Astro Image auto-WebP. Gallery captions embed property type + suburb + material for three-query ranking on each image.
Impact: Image Search traffic, accessibility compliance, Lighthouse 90+.
06
NAP lock + brand consistency + typo-free copy
Single canonical phone number in one include file. Single brand string with single case style applied site-wide. Copy passes grammar review before every deploy.
Impact: Clean local-SEO signal to Google, consistent SERP presentation, zero trust-eroding typos.
